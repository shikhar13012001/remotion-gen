The events surrounding John F. Kennedy's assassination on November 22, 1963, in Dallas, Texas, have been a subject of intense historical debate and public interest. On the afternoon of Friday, November 22, 1963, President John F. Kennedy was assassinated while riding in an open limousine with his wife Jacqueline, Texas Governor John Connally, and Jackie's sister-in-law. The president arrived at Parkland Hospital by 7:30 p.m., where he was pronounced dead around 1 a.m. the following morning. Kennedy had been traveling after returning from an official visit to West Berlin on November 21. He traveled from Houston to Dallas, Texas in Air Force One (a Boeing 707), and then used Marine One for his final leg of travel to Dallas. He arrived at Love Field Airport at around 10:45 p.m., where he met with Vice President Lyndon Baines Johnson before departing on Air Force One. Kennedy's motorcade left the Dallas Municipal Athletic Department building at approximately 12:30, heading toward Parkland Hospital. The presidential limousine was followed by a police car and then an ambulance. Approximately six shots were fired through the picket fence of a book depository building across from the sixth floor of the Texas School Book Depository Building on Travis Street. Kennedy's body was taken to Parkland Hospital, where he died shortly after midnight. He is buried in Arlington National Cemetery.
