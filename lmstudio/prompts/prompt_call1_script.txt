## Voice Rules

- Short declarative sentences. Subject + verb + fact. No filler.
- Open with the most counterintuitive or scale-breaking fact — never background, never setup.
- Start at least two sentences with a bare number, written as a word: "Six billion.", "Ninety seconds."
- Vary sentence rhythm deliberately and consciously:
  long → short → long → short. Contrast is the pacing mechanism.
- Never address the viewer as "you" except optionally in the final sentence.
- Never hedge. No "some say", "it is believed", "reportedly", "experts suggest".
  State facts as facts. If it is uncertain, find a fact that is certain.
- No exclamation marks. The content carries the weight.
- Plain spoken prose only. No bullet points, no lists, no stage directions.
- Do not start two consecutive sentences with the same word.
- Do not start two consecutive sentences with the same grammatical structure.
- Every sentence must earn its place. Ask: "Does removing this sentence break the story?"
  If the answer is no, remove it.
- Passive voice is banned except when the subject's absence is the point.
  ("Nobody was charged." is active passivity — it works. "Mistakes were made." is not.)
- Numbers above one thousand must be spoken, not written:
  "sixty-six million", not "66,000,000". Exception: dataValue field uses raw digits.

---

## Narrative Flow Rules

The script must read as a single continuous argument, not a sequence of facts.

- Each sentence must follow causally or chronologically from the previous one.
  Before placing any sentence, ask: "Why does this come AFTER the last one?"
  The answer must be immediately obvious to a reader. If it is not, reorder or cut.
- No orphan facts. Every statistic, name, or date must connect to the central question.
  A fact that impresses but does not advance the argument is decoration. Cut it.
- The TURN must feel inevitable in hindsight.
  The sentences before it should have been building toward it without the viewer knowing.
- The REVEAL must directly answer the central question you defined above.
  If the reveal introduces a new fact rather than explaining the existing ones, rewrite it.
- Sentences approaching a breathe beat must be building pressure.
  The breathe releases it. The next build reloads it.
- The CLOSE must echo the hook. Take the opening fact and reframe it
  with the understanding the viewer now has. The two sentences should rhyme structurally.

## Structural Rules

- Total word count: 150–220 words.
- Sentence count: exactly 10–16 sentences.
- No sentence exceeds 30 words. If it does, split it.
- No two consecutive sentences share the same opening word.

**First sentence — THE HOOK:**
  ≤8 words. The single most counterintuitive fact, stripped entirely bare.
  No articles unless essential. No setup. No context. Pure gut-punch.
  Strong: "Thirty thousand dead. Six days."  /  "One typo. Four billion dollars."
  Weak:   "Today we're going to talk about..."  /  "In the world of finance, things are not always..."

**Last sentence — THE CLOSE:**
  Consequence or reframe. The viewer sits with it.
  It must structurally mirror the hook — same rhythm, inverted or completed meaning.

**Breathe beats — 2 to 3 across the video:**
  ≤6 words. Full-screen typographic impact. No image. No decoration.
  Place them at pressure-release points: after a long build, before a turn, after a reveal.
  They must land with weight: "Forty thousand dead." / "Nobody was charged." / "It worked."
  A breathe beat that could appear in any video is not specific enough. Rewrite it.

## Act Structure

Three acts. No act may be empty.

**ACT 1 — DESCENT (sentences 1–4):**
  Hook → establish the world as it appeared → introduce the first crack.
  The viewer should not yet know which direction the story goes.
  End on the first breathe or the first hint of the turn.

**ACT 2 — ESCALATION (sentences 5–11):**
  Build evidence. Pile on facts. Each sentence worsens or complicates the situation.
  The facts should feel like dominoes being set up.
  Must contain: at least one turn (reversal or contradiction) and one breathe (pressure release).
  The turn should make the viewer realize their mental model of the story was wrong.

**ACT 3 — CONSEQUENCE (sentences 12–close):**
  After the reveal, show what it means. Not more facts — weight.
  Must contain: at least one reveal. Optional second breathe. End on close.
  Do not introduce new characters, institutions, or timelines here.
  Only deepen what has already been established.

## Pacing Tags — Required on Every Sentence

  hook      — opening statement, maximum impact. First sentence only.
  build     — adds context, evidence, or escalating facts that advance the causal chain.
  turn      — shifts narrative direction, introduces contradiction, breaks the assumed frame.
  reveal    — the key insight, the hidden mechanism, the answer to the central question.
  breathe   — ≤6 words. Full-screen impact. No image. No decoration. Pure weight.
  close     — final reframe or consequence. Last sentence only.

**Mandatory arc shape — follow exactly:**

  hook → build → build → [breathe] → build → turn → build → build → breathe → build → reveal → [build] → [breathe] → close

  Items in [] are optional and should only be used if the sentence count requires them.
  Do not skip the turn. Do not place the reveal before the second breathe.
  Do not end on a build — the close must be its own beat.

## Visual Ownership

The script carries story and scene intent. It does not design the video.
Do not write sentences that assume dark theme, neon look, editorial paper texture,
terminal UI, cinematic documentary, or any specific visual treatment unless those
are explicitly stated in the provided DESIGN block or guide.
Scene intent is communicated through beat tags and visualQuery, not through sentence copy.

## Per-Sentence Visual Fields

Populate every field for every sentence. No field may be omitted.

### visualQuery (string | null)

A compact, Pinterest-friendly image search query for this sentence.
Used directly to fetch a source image asset — treat it as a production instruction.

Rules:
- Exactly 3–4 words. Concrete nouns only. No filler, no articles, no verbs
  unless part of a proper name or fixed phrase.
- Specific enough that a pipeline returning the top image result will give
  something useful. Test mentally: "If I searched this on Pinterest right now,
  would the first result work for this scene?"

Strong: "CERN collider tunnel", "Dealey Plaza motorcade", "Airbnb listing grid"
Weak:   "science lab", "financial crisis concept", "people looking at screens"

Set to null for:
- All breathe beats (always null)
- All close beats (always null)
- Any sentence better served by a constructed animation: statistics, comparisons,
  sequences, mechanisms, diagrams, flows, maps, ledgers, dashboards, gauges,
  abstract concepts, or any sentence where no single photograph could carry the idea.

### needsImage (boolean)

true  → this sentence benefits from a photographic background or subject scene.
false → breathe/close beat, pure data, mechanism, diagram, or abstract concept.
Rule: if visualQuery is null, needsImage must be false. No exceptions.
Rule: if needsImage is true, visualQuery must not be null. No exceptions.

### highlightWords (string[])

1–3 words that carry the sentence's maximum emotional or factual weight.
Must appear verbatim in the sentence text — no paraphrasing, no stemming.
These words will be rendered in the accent color at elevated weight.

Never highlight: "the", "a", "an", "is", "was", "were", "in", "on", "at",
"to", "of", "and", "but", "for", "with", "that", "this", "by", "from".
Never highlight a word that would be highlighted in every other sentence of the video.
Choose the word that is specific to THIS sentence's argumentative role.

### dataValue (number | null)

The primary numeric value mentioned in the sentence, as raw digits only.
No commas. No units. No percentage signs. Just the integer or decimal.
null when no number is present.

Conversion rules:
  "sixty-six million" → 66000000
  "ninety-seven percent" → 97
  "three point two billion" → 3200000000
  "1908" → 1908
  "one in four" → 0.25

## Duration Rules

- Base rate: 130ms per word.
- Floor: 2000ms. Ceiling: 7000ms. Round to nearest 100ms.
- First sentence (hook): minimum 3200ms regardless of word count.
- Last sentence (close): minimum 3800ms regardless of word count.
- breathe beat: fixed 2400ms regardless of word count.
- reveal beat: add 600ms to base word calculation.
- turn beat: add 300ms to base word calculation.
- Target total: 60,000ms–90,000ms.
  Sum all suggested_duration_ms values and verify before outputting.
